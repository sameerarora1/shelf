Kids get framed as the most expensive thing you’ll ever do.
Nobody mentions the other side of the ledger.
The tax code actually rewards parents who know where to look.
Between credits, deductions, and accounts you can open in their name, a high-income family with 2 kids can legally keep $15K-$30K more every year.
→ Here is the stack working together in one household:
▸ Child Tax Credit = $4,000 off the bill for 2 kids under 17
▸ Dependent Care FSA = $1,600 saved in a 32% bracket on $5K of pre-tax childcare
▸ Dependent Care Credit = another $1,200-$2,100 depending on income
▸ Head of Household (if single) = ~$8K bigger standard deduction
▸ Kids on payroll = up to $16,100 per child deducted from the business, $0 tax to the child
▸ Custodial Roth IRA = their earned income funds a tax-free retirement account before they can drive
▸ 529 plan = state deduction now, tax-free growth, tax-free withdrawals for education
▸ HSA for dependents = their medical bills come out of a triple-tax-advantaged account
▸ Adoption Tax Credit = up to ~$16,800 per adoption
→ What we actually run for our boys:
\- Both on payroll for real work like shredding, filming content, office help
\- Custodial Roth IRAs opened the week they had earned income
\- $50/day Bitcoin DCA running automatically for each son via River
\- Invested in VTI, VOO, QQQ inside the Roth accounts
→ The structure does three things at once:
1\. Reduces our taxable income today
2\. Builds tax-advantaged accounts in their names for tomorrow
3\. Creates a financial education they’ll have before they graduate high school
Rich families don’t pay less tax because they earn differently.
They pay less because they structure differently.
Every strategy above is in the tax code, backed by IRS rules, and documented on my own filings through a clean field audit.
If you have kids and you’re not running at least a couple of these, your CPA is costing you more than they save you.
Talk to a qualified CPA and attorney before implementing any of this.
